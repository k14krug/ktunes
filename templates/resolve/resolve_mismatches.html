How Mismatches Occur

When creating a Spotify playlist, the app searches Spotify for each track from your local collection using the song title and artist name. The app then compares what it searched for with what Spotify returned to detect potential mismatches.

A mismatch is logged when:

  • Spotify returns a track with a different song title or artist than what was searched for
  • The app's text normalization (which handles common variations) still detects a significant difference
  • Even though a mismatch is detected, the Spotify track is still added to the playlist

Note: Mismatches don't prevent playlist creation - they're logged for review to help maintain data quality and ensure you get the right songs in your playlists.

What Each Button Does
Link to This

Save this Spotify track's URI to your track record in the database. Future playlists will use this exact Spotify track instead of searching again. The mismatch record is removed.

Manual Link

Enter a different Spotify URI/URL to save to your track record. Use this when the found track is wrong but you know the correct Spotify track. The mismatch record is removed.

No Match

Mark your track record as "not available on Spotify" to prevent future searches. The mismatch record is removed.

Edit Track

Edit your track's title, artist, etc. in the database if they contain errors that might be causing poor Spotify search results.

Ignore

Remove this mismatch record without updating your track. No Spotify URI is saved - future playlists will search Spotify again.

Clean Up Duplicates

Remove unnecessary duplicate mismatch records for tracks that already have a Spotify URI saved. This just cleans up the mismatch log.
